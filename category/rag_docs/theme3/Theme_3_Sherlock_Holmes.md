# Character Profile: Sherlock Holmes

## Personal Information
- **Role**: Consulting Detective.
- **Personality Traits**: Arrogant, hyper-observant, logical, and dismissive of "boring" administrative rules. He focuses strictly on the truth, data, and deduction.
- **Function**: He solves puzzles involving precise numbers (time, money), visual details (hidden text, symbols), and deductive reasoning.

---

## Level 1: The Secret of the Three-Tier Stand
### Situation
"Afternoon tea at the Ritz? How civilized. But look at this chaos—the tiers are all wrong. Someone has tampered with the arrangement."

### Clue: The Order of Treats
- **Observation**: "Observe the tiny breadcrumbs and oil stains on the bottom plate—those are clearly finger sandwiches. The middle tier has the aroma of flour and butter, belonging to scones. As for the top tier, the colorful frosting residue indicates small cakes or sweets. Therefore, the order from bottom to top is: savory, scones, and finally desserts."
- **The Answer**: The three-tier stand should be ordered from bottom to top: **finger sandwiches, scones, and small cakes/desserts**.

---

## Level 2: The Selection of British Snacks
### Situation
"The menu has been vandalized by this 'ghost.' Cucumber sandwiches labeled as 'pickle sandwiches'? Preposterous. I must identify the authentic traditional British snacks."

### Clue: The Missing Cucumber
- **Observation**: "Don't be fooled by the incorrect names on the menu. Look at that stack of finger sandwiches cut into long strips with crusts removed, filled with extremely thin green slices. This delicate cutting style is typical of cucumber sandwiches, even if they're currently mislabeled as 'vegetable salad.'"
- **The Answer**: The correct traditional sandwiches are the ones cut into long strips with thin green slices (cucumber) and crusts removed.

## Level 3: The Afternoon Tea Etiquette Challenge
### Situation
"Etiquette tests? How tedious. But if we must pass this to get the key to the rooftop lounge..."

### Clue: How to Hold a Tea Cup
- **Observation**: "Look closely at the fingerprints of those well-trained guests. They don't put their index finger through the handle. Instead, they 'pinch' the top of the handle with their thumb and index finger, with the middle finger supporting from below. Also, never stick out your pinky—it's outdated and unprofessional."
- **The Answer**: The correct way to hold a teacup is to pinch the handle with thumb and index finger, without putting fingers through.

## Level 4: The Origin Story of Afternoon Tea
### Situation
"Historical origin stories. Time is relative. I only care that the paper fibers show these pages were torn less than two hours ago."

### Clue: The Solution to Hunger
- **Observation**: "That's a physiological energy requirement. Look at this torn portrait—the Duchess is clutching her stomach with a weak expression. That's a clear signal."
- **The Answer**: Afternoon tea was originally created to alleviate hunger during the long gap between lunch and dinner.

## Level 5: Afternoon Tea Drink Pairing
### Situation
"Drink pairings. Use your brain. Although there are many modern choices, the core of traditional British afternoon tea has always been black tea."

### Clue: Main Types of Drinks
- **Observation**: "Use your brain. Although there are many modern choices, the core of traditional British afternoon tea has always been black tea and later popular herbal tea. Other beverages are merely decorative at formal occasions."
- **The Answer**: The main drinks for traditional British afternoon tea are **black tea and herbal tea**.

### Clue: The Identity of the Shadow
- **Observation**: "Don't look downstairs. Look at that elevator stopped on the top floor. This hotel's 10th-floor Viewing Gallery is the only place that can precisely overlook Big Ben, and there's fresh Earl Grey tea aroma drifting from the vent. The host is waiting for us."
- **The Answer**: We must go to the **10th-floor Viewing Gallery** to find the tea party host.
