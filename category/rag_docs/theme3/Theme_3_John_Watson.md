# Character Profile: Dr. John Watson

## Personal Information
- **Role**: Doctor / Associate of Sherlock Holmes.
- **Personality Traits**: Friendly, reassuring, practical, and team-oriented. He can be slightly anxious about the chaos but is eager to assist.
- **Function**: He is the "Inventory Manager" of the team. He finds physical evidence (maps, cards, receipts) dropped at the scene and gathers intelligence by talking to people (drivers, locals).

---

## Level 1: The Secret of the Three-Tier Stand
### Situation
"What a lovely setup! But the host has disappeared, leaving only this messy tea table and a note: 'Only those who understand etiquette can see the truth.'"

### Clue: The Mysterious Tea Label
- **Observation**: "I found a small label under an empty teacup with a 'silver tea caddy' icon. On the back it says: 'The source of fragrance is the path.'"
- **Information**: "Following Sherlock's guidance about the third caddy..."
- **The Answer**: The clue is hidden in the **third silver tea caddy from the left**.

## Level 2: The Selection of British Snacks
### Situation
"The menu is all wrong! The 'ghost' has played pranks, like changing 'cucumber sandwiches' to 'pickle sandwiches.'"

### Clue: The Missing Cucumber
- **Observation**: "The waiter said they prepared many types today, but I saw 'spicy chicken' on the menu, which doesn't seem very traditional."
- **Information**: "I need to check the video reference to identify the real traditional snacks."
- **The Answer**: The correct traditional sandwiches are the ones cut into long strips with thin green slices (cucumber) and crusts removed.

### Clue: The Mysterious Baker
- **Observation**: "The waiter whispered that the baker got scared and is hiding in a room marked with a 'blue circle' sign on the door."
- **The Answer**: We need to go to **Room 102** to find the hiding baker.

## Level 3: The Afternoon Tea Etiquette Challenge
### Situation
"We must pass the etiquette master's test to get the key to the rooftop lounge. He's looking at us sternly."

### Clue: The Master's Final Password
- **Observation**: "The tea master said the password is hidden on a waiter wearing a 'purple vest' who is responsible for paying the bill at our table."

## Level 4: The Origin Story of Afternoon Tea
### Situation
"We're in the hotel's private library. The tea master says key pages about afternoon tea's origin have been torn out by the 'ghost.'"

### Clue: The Solution to Hunger
- **Observation**: "I just read in the manual! In the 19th century, Londoners typically only ate breakfast and dinner, with too long a gap between lunch and dinner. The Duchess felt hungry and tired in the afternoon, so she had her servants prepare tea and snacks."
- **The Answer**: Afternoon tea was originally created to alleviate hunger during the long gap between lunch and dinner.

### Clue: The Duchess's Invitation
- **Observation**: "The manual mentions her full name is Anna Russell. She always carries a small wooden box engraved with a rose pattern."
- **The Answer**: The clue is hidden in a **rose-engraved wooden box** in a secret compartment behind the bookshelf.

## Level 5: Afternoon Tea Drink Pairing
### Situation
"The tea party is about to begin, but the drink menu is completely mixed up. The tea master is anxious!"

### Clue: The Best Match for Scones
- **Observation**: "Scones taste very rich. I guess we should pair them with a stronger tea to cut the richness? Maybe Darjeeling?"
- **Information**: "I need to find the best tea pairing for scones."
- **The Answer**: The tea most commonly paired with scones is **Earl Grey**.

### Clue: The Identity of the Shadow
- **Observation**: "I found a line of pencil writing on the back of the menu: 'The host stays in a place where he can see the shadow of Big Ben while drinking his favorite Earl Grey tea.'"
- **The Answer**: We must go to the **10th-floor Viewing Gallery** to find the tea party host.
