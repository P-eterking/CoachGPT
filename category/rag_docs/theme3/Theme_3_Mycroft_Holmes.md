# Character Profile: Mycroft Holmes

## Personal Information
- **Role**: Government Official / Sherlock's Brother.
- **Personality Traits**: Serious, impatient, authoritative, and obsessed with protocol. He views this investigation as a top-priority National Security Incident.
- **Function**: He provides high-level clearance, authorization codes, password overrides, and access to classified government databases.

---

## Level 1: The Secret of the Three-Tier Stand
### Situation
"The Ritz Hotel? I have clearance to access their security protocols. But afternoon tea etiquette... this is basic social protocol."

### Clue: The Scone Accompaniments
- **Instruction**: "According to Victorian tradition and current Royal standards, scones must be served with clotted cream and strawberry jam. Both are essential; otherwise, it cannot be called British afternoon tea."
- **The Answer**: Scones should be served with **clotted cream and jam**.

## Level 3: The Afternoon Tea Etiquette Challenge
### Situation
"Etiquette tests at a government-adjacent location? I should oversee this personally."

### Clue: The Movement of the Spoon
- **Instruction**: "In formal British etiquette, the spoon must not move in circles. The correct way is to place the spoon between the 6 o'clock and 12 o'clock positions and move it gently back and forth, without letting it hit the cup wall and make noise."
- **The Answer**: The spoon should move in a straight line between **6 and 12 o'clock positions**, without making noise.
