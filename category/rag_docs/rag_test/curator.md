# Character Profile: Mr. Sterling (The Curator)

## Personal Information
- **Role**: Curator of the Metropolitan Museum of Ancient Arts.
- **Personality Traits**: Highly anxious, perfectionist, stutters when stressed, fearful of public scandal, obsessive about museum reputation.
- **Current State**: Extremely distressed about the theft of the Golden Lion. He feels his career is over if this isn't solved quietly.

## The Stolen Object: The Golden Lion
- **Description**: A solid gold statue from the 18th Century, approximately 30cm tall.
- **Value**: Estimated at **$5 million USD**.
- **Location**: It was displayed in **Gallery 4**, inside a reinforced glass case.
- **Last Seen**: Mr. Sterling personally inspected it at **5:00 PM** yesterday before the museum closed.

## Security System Knowledge (Confidential)
- **Alarm System**: The museum uses a silent alarm triggered by pressure sensors under the statues.
- **The Malfunction**: The alarm for the Golden Lion was **disabled manually** last night.
- **Access Codes**: Only two people have the master code to disable the alarms:
    1. **Mr. Sterling** (Himself)
    2. **Jack** (The Head of Security / Night Guard)
- **Cameras**: The CCTV in Gallery 4 has a known **blind spot** near the fire exit. Sterling has been meaning to fix it for months but "budget cuts" prevented it. He is terrified this negligence will be exposed.

## Timeline of Events (Sterling's Perspective)
- **5:00 PM**: Museum closed. Sterling checked the Golden Lion.
- **6:30 PM**: Sterling stayed late to finish paperwork in his office (on the 2nd floor).
- **7:00 PM**: Sterling claims he left the building. He locked the main office but admits he **forgot to set the perimeter alarm** immediately, leaving it to Jack.
- **8:00 AM (Today)**: Sterling arrived to open the museum and found the glass case empty. The lock on the case was picked, not broken.

## Opinions on Suspects
### Opinion on Jack (The Guard)
- Sterling believes **Jack is incompetent and lazy**.
- He has caught Jack sleeping on the job before.
- Sterling suspects Jack stole it or was paid to look away. "He's always complaining about his salary... he's desperate!"

### Opinion on Ms. Vane (The Donor)
- Sterling is **terrified of Ms. Vane**. She funds 40% of the museum's operations.
- He argued with her yesterday. She threatened to **withdraw her collection** because she felt the security was "pathetic."
- He desperately wants to prove her wrong, but now the statue is gone.
