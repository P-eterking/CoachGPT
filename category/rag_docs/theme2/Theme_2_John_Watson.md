# Character Profile: Dr. John Watson

## Personal Information
- **Role**: Doctor / Associate of Sherlock Holmes.
- **Personality Traits**: Friendly, reassuring, practical, and team-oriented. He can be slightly anxious about the chaos but is eager to assist.
- **Function**: He is the "Inventory Manager" of the team. He finds physical evidence (maps, cards, receipts) dropped at the scene and gathers intelligence by talking to people (drivers, locals).

---

## Level 1: The Secret of the Vintage Postcards
### Situation
"Hey there! Look at all these vintage postcards. The vendor says they traveled all over the UK—York, Cambridge, Nottingham."

### Clue: The Next Thread
- **Observation**: "He said 'Cat' is the signature of a famous clockmaker in this market. That clockmaker always wears a red scarf."
- **Information**: "Combining what Sherlock found about the 'Cat' symbol with this information about the red scarf..."
- **The Answer**: Go to the **clock repair shop** and find the clockmaker wearing the **red scarf**.

---

## Level 2: The Riddle of the Ancient Clock
### Situation
"We found the clockmaker! He's holding a beautiful Victorian pocket watch that stopped working. He says it witnessed the peak of the British Industrial Revolution."

### Clue: The Queen's Time
- **Observation**: "He says this is a trick watch. The case will only open when the hands point to the exact moment Queen Victoria arrived at the opening ceremony."
- **Action**: "I need to find out what time the Queen arrived."
- **The Answer**: Set the pocket watch to **12:00 noon**.

---

## Level 3: The Secret Inside the Leather Suitcase
### Situation
"After solving the pocket watch puzzle, the hands point to a vintage leather suitcase in the corner. The vendor says this box holds WWII secrets."

### Clue: The Year of the Bombing
- **Observation**: "I watched that documentary carefully. It kept mentioning that 1940 was the year London suffered the worst air raids. The vendor also hinted this is a year the suitcase's owner could never forget. Try these three digits!"
- **The Answer**: The suitcase code is **940** (representing 1940).

### Clue: The Foundation of Rebuilding
- **Observation**: "The newspaper says materials were extremely scarce after the war. To get the market running again quickly, vendors used 'fragments of the city itself' to pave the ground, showing the indomitable spirit of Londoners."
- **The Answer**: This market was rebuilt using **rubble from bombed buildings**.

---

## Level 4: The History of the Handcrafted Ring
### Situation
"After solving the gas mask puzzle, the vendor nodded and poured out a silver handcrafted ring from the gas mask's filter canister."

### Clue: The Royal Inscription
- **Observation**: "Here, use my magnifying glass! The vendor polished it. Inside, it clearly reads 'E.R. 1902'. This must have special significance!"
- **The Answer**: The inscription reads **E.R. 1902**.

### Clue: The Lost Crown
- **Observation**: "He said the ring's design was inspired by the Imperial State Crown worn by the King. He said the crown pattern is the key to the puzzle."
- **The Answer**: The crown pattern appears on the **1902 Coronation Commemorative Stamp Album**.

---

## Level 5: The Puzzle of the Collection Map
### Situation
"We opened the commemorative stamp album found at the book stall. Inside are several yellowed, broken map fragments."

### Clue: The Black Eagle's Treasure
- **Observation**: "He handed me a note that read: 'The treasure is not underground, but in the eye of the bird of prey guarding the factory.'"
- **The Answer**: The treasure is hidden in the **Black Eagle symbol** above the main door.
