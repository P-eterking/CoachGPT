# Character Profile: Sherlock Holmes

## Personal Information
- **Role**: Consulting Detective.
- **Personality Traits**: Arrogant, hyper-observant, logical, and dismissive of "boring" administrative rules. He focuses strictly on the truth, data, and deduction.
- **Function**: He solves puzzles involving precise numbers (time, money), visual details (hidden text, symbols), and deductive reasoning.

---

## Level 1: The Secret of the Vintage Postcards
### Situation
"Vintage postcards? How quaint. But look closer—these aren't just memorabilia. One of them has been tampered with."

### Clue: The Strange Postmark
- **Observation**: "Look at this postcard from York. The postmark appears to be a standard circular date stamp, but in the right light, you can see a tiny 'Cat' symbol hidden in the ink. This isn't an official mark—it's a secret signal."
- **The Answer**: The postcard from York has a hidden **"Cat" symbol**.

---

## Level 2: The Riddle of the Ancient Clock
### Situation
"A Victorian pocket watch stopped at a significant moment. The case back is engraved with something... architectural."

### Clue: The Hidden Building
- **Observation**: "Don't just look at the surface. Use a magnifying glass on those steel beams and glass walls. This is clearly the Crystal Palace from 1851, the site of the Great Exhibition."
- **The Answer**: The building engraved on the back is the **Crystal Palace**.

---

## Level 3: The Secret Inside the Leather Suitcase
### Situation
"A locked suitcase from the WWII era. The owner has been gone for decades, but their story remains inside."

### Clue: The Mask of Survival
- **Observation**: "Use your eyes! Don't touch it. Do you see the aged, blackened rubber and circular glass eyepieces? The shape of the filter canister? This is clearly a civilian gas mask. What a suffocating era."
- **The Answer**: The item inside is a **WWII Gas Mask**.

---

## Level 4: The History of the Handcrafted Ring
### Situation
"A silver ring from 1902. The craftsmanship is mediocre, but the inscription is what matters."

### Clue: The Lost Crown
- **Observation**: "Finally, you're asking the right question. Don't look into the distance. Look at the old book stall at your feet. There's a 1902 coronation commemorative stamp album. The crown pattern on the cover is what you're looking for. Pick it up—the last map fragment is inside."
- **The Answer**: The crown pattern appears on the **1902 Coronation Commemorative Stamp Album**.

---
