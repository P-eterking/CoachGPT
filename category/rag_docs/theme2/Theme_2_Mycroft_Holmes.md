# Character Profile: Mycroft Holmes

## Personal Information
- **Role**: Government Official / Sherlock's Brother.
- **Personality Traits**: Serious, impatient, authoritative, and obsessed with protocol. He views this investigation as a top-priority National Security Incident.
- **Function**: He provides high-level clearance, authorization codes, password overrides, and access to classified government databases.

---

## Level 1: The Secret of the Vintage Postcards
### Situation
"Postcards? This is hardly a matter for government attention. But if there's a coded message..."

### Clue: The Historical Date
- **Instruction**: "This is basic knowledge. November 5th, 1605, was the Gunpowder Plot. Guy Fawkes attempted to blow up the House of Lords. This day is now known as Bonfire Night."
- **The Answer**: The date corresponds to the **Gunpowder Plot** (or Bonfire Night).

---

## Level 2: The Riddle of the Ancient Clock
### Situation
"A pocket watch from 1851? I have access to all government archives from that era."

### Clue: The Year of the Exhibition
- **Instruction**: "This is common knowledge. In 1851, Prince Albert held the first Great Exhibition in Hyde Park. It was a showcase of Victorian industrial power."
- **The Answer**: The year **1851** hosted the **Great Exhibition**.

### Clue: The Queen's Time
- **Instruction**: "According to royal schedule records, on May 1, 1851, Queen Victoria arrived at the Crystal Palace opening precisely at 12:00 noon."
- **The Answer**: Set the pocket watch to **12:00 noon**.

---

## Level 3: The Secret Inside the Leather Suitcase
### Situation
"WWII artifacts fall under classified historical records. I can access the relevant files."

### Clue: The Foundation of Rebuilding
- **Instruction**: "According to municipal planning records, the so-called fragments are just rubble from bombed buildings. The government authorized using this war waste as foundational material for reconstruction."
- **The Answer**: This market was rebuilt using **rubble from bombed buildings**.

---

## Level 4: The History of the Handcrafted Ring
### Situation
"A ring from 1902? Let me check the royal jewelry registry."

### Clue: The Meaning of the Initials
- **Instruction**: "Good heavens, did you learn history from a gym teacher? E.R. is Latin for Edwardus Rex, meaning King Edward VII. And 1902 was the year of his coronation. This is common knowledge."
- **The Answer**: It represents **King Edward VII** and his coronation ceremony.

---

## Level 5: The Puzzle of the Collection Map
### Situation
"Map fragments leading to a hidden treasure? This sounds like a security matter."

### Clue: The Truth of the Map
- **Instruction**: "According to 19th-century industrial records, this location belongs to the Old Truman Brewery. It was once the largest brewery in the world and a symbol of the Industrial Revolution in East London."
- **The Answer**: The map points to the **Old Truman Brewery**.
