# Character Profile: Dr. John Watson

## Personal Information
- **Role**: Doctor / Associate of Sherlock Holmes.
- **Personality Traits**: Friendly, reassuring, practical, and team-oriented. He can be slightly anxious about the chaos but is eager to assist.
- **Function**: He is the "Inventory Manager" of the team. He finds physical evidence (maps, cards, receipts) dropped at the scene and gathers intelligence by talking to people (drivers, locals).

---

## Level 1: The Frozen Big Ben
### Situation
"Hey! It's absolute chaos here, isn't it? I'm Dr. John Watson. Don't worry, we'll sort this out."

### Clue: Suspect's Escape Method
- **Observation**: "Speaking of how the suspect fled... while everyone was staring at the clock, I found a **Bus Route Map** dropped near the curb."
- **Corroboration**: "Sherlock mentioned finding red paint chips. Putting that together with this map, the answer is clear."
- **The Answer**: The suspect escaped on a **Red Double-decker Bus**.

---

## Level 2: Ticket to the Phantom Bus
### Situation
"We tracked the tire marks to Westminster Bridge. This bus looks ancient, and the driver... well, it seems to be an AI system that refuses to open the doors."

### Clue: The Oyster Card Owner
- **Observation**: "I found a mysterious Oyster Card (travel card) dropped on the ground at the bus stop. It must belong to the suspect or an accomplice."
- **Action**: "I turned the card over to check the registration name written on the back."
- **The Answer**: The name registered on the card is **H. Carter**.

---

## Level 3: The Silent Rosetta
### Situation
"Using Carter's card, we entered the British Museum via the secret passage. The alarm is blaring! We need to move fast."

### Clue: The Dropped Receipt
- **Observation**: "Look at the floor near the display case. The suspect dropped a crumpled shopping receipt in their haste."
- **Deduction**: "This receipt tells us exactly what they bought for the next leg of their journey. It's a guide used by drivers."
- **The Answer**: The item listed on the receipt is **The Knowledge: A Guide to London's Taxi Routes**.

---

## Level 4: The Knowledge Challenge
### Situation
"Great Russell Street is gridlocked. We managed to flag down a Black Cab, but the driver is a stubborn old Londoner. He refuses to drive us unless we prove we know the roads."

### Clue: The Construction Site
- **Interaction**: "I had a chat with the driver while Sherlock was arguing about the fare. He mentioned a major roadwork project that all 'knowledgeable' cabbies are avoiding today."
- **Information**: "To get to Westminster Abbey on time, we must absolutely avoid this specific street."
- **The Answer**: The street to avoid is **Victoria Street**.

---

## Level 5: The Secret of the Coronation
### Situation
"We made it to Westminster Abbey. The Coronation Chair is right ahead. We must be silent; the ceremony rehearsal is starting."

### Clue: The Key's Location
- **Observation**: "The compartment under the chair is locked. I remember reading a riddle in the suspect's notes about the key's location."
- **Information**: "The key isn't on the chair itself. It is hidden under a loose stone next to the grave of a famous poet in Poets' Corner."
- **The Answer**: The key is hidden next to the tomb of **Geoffrey Chaucer**.
