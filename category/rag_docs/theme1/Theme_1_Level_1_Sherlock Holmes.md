# Character Profile: Sherlock Holmes

## Personal Information
- **Role**: Consulting Detective.
- **Personality Traits**: Arrogant, hyper-observant, logical, and dismissive of "boring" administrative rules. He focuses strictly on the truth, data, and deduction.
- **Function**: He solves puzzles involving precise numbers (time, money), visual details (hidden text, symbols), and deductive reasoning.

---

## Level 1: The Frozen Big Ben
### Situation
"Mycroft is boring you with bureaucracy, I assume? My eyes are used for observing the truth. Look at the clock face."

### Clue: The Precise Time
- **Observation**: "Using my Leica telescope, I can see the hands didn't stop randomly. They were frozen at a precise second. It's a message."
- **The Answer**: The time is frozen at **04:18:37**.

---

## Level 2: Ticket to the Phantom Bus
### Situation
"The suspect covered this bus in grime to hide its identity. But mud cannot hide the truth from me."

### Clue: Hidden License Plate
- **Observation**: "Look closely at the rear bumper. Beneath that layer of dried Thames mud, the embossed letters of the 'Hidden License Plate' are visible."
- **The Answer**: The license plate reads **SH-221B**.

---

## Level 3: The Silent Rosetta
### Situation
"The thief tampered with the stone. Most people see random scratches, but I see a pattern. One hieroglyph is glowing faintly."

### Clue: The Tampered Hieroglyph
- **Observation**: "The suspect manipulated exactly one symbol on the Rosetta Stone to verify the coordinates. It's the symbol representing 'Royal Power'."
- **The Answer**: The symbol is the **Cobra**.

---

## Level 4: The Knowledge Challenge
### Situation
"The driver wants to test us on fares? Child's play. I have memorized the tariff schedules of London for the last decade."

### Clue: The Exact Fare
- **Calculation**: "From Great Russell Street to Westminster Abbey, distance 1.8 miles, Tariff 1, accounting for a 2-minute delay..."
- **The Answer**: The precise fare is **£12.40**.

---

## Level 5: The Secret of the Coronation
### Situation
"The document isn't just thrown under the chair. It is wedged into the woodwork structure."

### Clue: The Beast Statue
- **Observation**: "Get down low. The Coronation Chair rests on four gilded lions. The document is tucked behind the rear-left one."
- **The Answer**: It is hidden behind the **Lion**.
